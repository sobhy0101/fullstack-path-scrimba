My new journey as a bootcamp student

After several months of learning in the Frontend Developer Career Path, I've made the big jump over to the Scrimba Bootcamp to get expert code reviews of my Solo Projects projects and meet like-minded peers.

How I stay committed to learning

I like to think of myself as a lifelong learner. I used to spend hours and hours learning, then try to create simple projects using what I learned. That would have been great if those projects actually helped me learn what I was trying to learn. But in reality, I wasn't spending enough time figuring out how to integrate different concepts together.

Thankfully, I was able to learn an alternative approach to learning. Instead of coding all day, I started spending just 30 minutes a day on projects, and the rest of my time I would spend actively practicing what I learned through building small components and refactoring code.

How I got started

I started simple and gradually grew my learning plan. I would decide on a single concept to focus on each day - maybe Grid layouts, or Flexbox alignment, or responsive images. Then I'd build something small that specifically used that concept.

The breakthrough for me was documenting everything in this learning journal. By teaching others what I'm learning, I solidify my own understanding. Plus, it's a great way to build a portfolio that shows my growth as a developer.

The power of community

Joining the Scrimba Bootcamp has been transformative. Getting feedback from experienced developers and connecting with fellow learners has accelerated my progress in ways I never imagined. We review each other's code, share tips, and celebrate wins together.

If you're on the fence about joining a bootcamp or learning community, my advice is simple: do it. The accountability and support system is worth its weight in gold.
